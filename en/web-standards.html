Government of Canada IPv6 Adoption Strategy

A new version of the Internet Protocol, called IPv6, has been standardized to provide a solution to the issue of IPv4 address depletion.

  • Unlike some technology advances, IPv6 was not designed to be backwards compatible with its predecessor IPv4. Network operators, content owners and service providers may need to support both IPv4 and IPv6 in their infrastructures for a number of years to assure the continuity and interoperability of Internet users and services.
  • The Government of Canada recognizes that it must adopt IPv6 not only to maintain business continuity but also to continue growing and enhancing online services for Canadian citizens, businesses and public servants. Adopting IPv6 will also enable students, visitors, investors and business partners in other countries to easily find information about Canada on GC websites.

The first phase is under way and is expected to be completed by the end of September 2013. The second phase will focus on the IPv6 enablement of the principal GC externally-facing websites and is expected to be completed by the end of March 2015.

Standard on Privacy and Web Analytics

Government institutions using Web analytics externally on servers hosted by third parties must comply with the requirements of Appendix A of this standard as of January 31, 2013, with the exception of those requirements in section 3 of Appendix A, which must be complied with no later than June 30, 2014. This standard takes effect on January 31, 2013.

Supplemental documentation to assist institutions in implementing the requirements under the new Standard has been developed:

  • Directive on Privacy Practices
  • A Privacy Impact Assessment for Web Analytics
  • An information sheet in the form of Frequently Asked Questions
  • A sample Privacy Notice to help meet the notice requirements under the Standard

Standard on Web Interoperability

This standard takes effect on July 1, 2012.

Section 6.1 is to be implemented in phases and applies as follows:

  • Phase I (July 1, 2012 – June 30, 2013) — One requirement of the standard
  • Phase II (April 1, 2013 – December 31, 2014) — Two more requirements, for a total of three requirements of the standard
  • Phase III (January 1, 2015 – June 30, 2015) — All four requirements of the standard
